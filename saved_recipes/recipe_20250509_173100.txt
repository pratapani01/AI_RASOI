Ingredients: chicken, rice
Spice Level: Medium
Region: North Indian
Meal Type: Lunch

Recipe:
1. Recipe Name:  Saffron Chicken & Brown Rice with Jeera Aloo

2. Ingredients Needed:

* 250 gms boneless chicken breast, cubed
* 1 cup brown rice
* 1 medium onion, finely chopped
* 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
* 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
* 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
* 1 tsp turmeric powder
* 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 1/4 tsp saffron strands, soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
* 1 tbsp ghee or olive oil
* Salt to taste
* Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish
* **For Jeera Aloo (cumin potatoes):**
    * 2 medium potatoes, cubed and boiled
    * 1 tsp cumin seeds
    * 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    * 1/4 tsp red chili powder
    * Salt to taste
    * 1 tbsp ghee or olive oil


3. Step-by-Step Instructions:

* **Prepare the Chicken:** Heat ghee/oil in a pan. Add onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.  Add chicken and cook until lightly browned. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.  Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
* **Add Vegetables & Spices:** Add tomatoes and green peas. Cook until tomatoes soften. Stir in garam masala.
* **Simmer & Saffron:** Add soaked saffron milk. Mix well and simmer for 10-12 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
* **Cook the Rice:** While the chicken simmers, cook brown rice according to package instructions.
* **Make Jeera Aloo:** In a separate pan, heat ghee/oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add boiled potatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until potatoes are lightly coated with spices.
* **Serve:** Serve the saffron chicken over a bed of brown rice, alongside the Jeera Aloo. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.


4. Estimated Calories: Approximately 450-500 calories per serving (depending on portion size and oil used).

5. Cuisine Type: North Indian

6. Dietary Info: Gluten-free, relatively low in fat (if olive oil is used), good source of protein and fiber. Can be easily adapted for vegetarian diet by replacing chicken with paneer or tofu.
